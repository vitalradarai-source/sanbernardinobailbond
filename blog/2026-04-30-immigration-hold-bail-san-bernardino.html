An immigration hold in San Bernardino complicates an already stressful situation. Two completely separate legal systems run simultaneously — California criminal law and federal immigration law. San Bernardino's proximity to the Adelanto ICE Processing Center (~70 miles) makes transfer risk particularly real. This guide explains what an ICE hold is, what it means for bail, and the critical steps to take before posting any bond.

This article is general information, not legal advice. ICE hold cases require both a criminal defense attorney and an immigration attorney. Do not make decisions based solely on this guide.

What an ICE Detainer Actually Is

An ICE detainer — officially Form I-247A — is a request from U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement to a local jail. It asks the jail to hold a person up to 48 hours past their criminal release time so ICE can take them into federal custody for removal proceedings.

Important: a detainer is a request, not a court-ordered warrant. Local jails decide whether to honor it.

California's SB 54 — How San Bernardino County Applies It

California's Senate Bill 54 (California Values Act, 2017) restricts local cooperation with ICE. It prohibits state and local law enforcement from honoring ICE detainers except for:

  • Defendants with serious or violent felony convictions within the past 15 years (PC §1192.7 or §667.5)
  • Current charges that qualify as serious or violent felonies
  • Specific aggravated felonies (sex crimes against minors, gang-related crimes)
  • Federal warrants signed by a judge (different from a detainer)

San Bernardino Sheriff's Department applies SB 54 — for most misdemeanors and lower-level felonies, SBSD will NOT hold the defendant past their criminal release time. However, ICE can arrest separately at the courthouse, on the way home, or in the community.

San Bernardino-specific risk: The Adelanto ICE Processing Center is only ~70 miles north of San Bernardino. ICE has historically prioritized Inland Empire transfers due to proximity. Even a successful state bail can lead directly to Adelanto custody.

Three Possible Outcomes When You Post Bail With an ICE Hold

  1. SB 54 protects release: Detainer is for an offense not covered by SB 54. Defendant released to family per criminal release process.
  2. SB 54 allows transfer: Detainer covers serious felony or qualifying past conviction. Defendant transfers to ICE custody at Adelanto.
  3. ICE arrest in the community: Defendant released from jail but ICE arrests at courthouse exit, on the way home, or later.

The bail premium (10% under California Insurance Code §1800.4) is non-refundable in all three scenarios. If the defendant ends up in ICE custody and misses a state court date, the bondsman may file for bond exoneration under PC §1305, but this requires legal process.

Critical Steps Before Posting Bail With an ICE Hold

  1. Confirm the ICE detainer. Call the booking facility (SBSD Central Detention Center, (909) 387-3545) and ask whether an ICE detainer has been filed. Get the detainer number if possible.
  2. Hire a criminal defense attorney. They handle the underlying state charge.
  3. Hire an immigration attorney. Federal removal proceedings — separate specialty. Free legal aid:
    • Inland Empire Latino Lawyers Association: (909) 381-7177 — Inland Empire-specific
    • TODEC Legal Center: (951) 943-1955 — immigrant rights services
    • Public Counsel Immigrant Rights Project: (213) 385-2977
    • Immigrant Defenders Law Center (ImmDef): (213) 634-0999
  4. Discuss strategy. Sometimes posting bail isn't the right move. If ICE will likely take custody anyway, fighting the criminal case from custody (where ICE can't easily transfer mid-case) may be better than posting and ending up in Adelanto.
  5. Understand the financial exposure. 10% premium non-refundable. Collateral pledged may be at risk if defendant misses court due to ICE detention.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an ICE detainer?
Form I-247A — federal request asking the local jail to hold someone up to 48 hours past their criminal release time for ICE custody. A request, not a court warrant.
Does San Bernardino cooperate with ICE?
SBSD applies SB 54 — honors detainers only for serious/violent felonies. Most misdemeanors not held. ICE can still arrest in the community separately.
Can I bail someone out with an ICE hold?
Yes — bail can be posted. But ICE may take custody if SB 54 exceptions apply. Adelanto is only ~70 miles away — transfer risk is real. Always consult both attorneys.
What happens if ICE takes them after I post bail?
10% premium non-refundable per CA Ins Code §1800.4. Defendant transfers to Adelanto. Missed state court dates may require bond exoneration filings under PC §1305.
What should families do first?
Hire BOTH criminal defense AND immigration attorney (Inland Empire Latino Lawyers, TODEC, Public Counsel, ImmDef). Confirm detainer with SBSD. Discuss whether posting bail is strategically correct.
